<h1>
Hydrodynamical simulation movie
</h1>
<p>Movie of the three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulation performed with mass ratio <i>q</i> = 0.1, upstream Mach number <i>M</i><sub>&infin;</sub> = 1.69 in the <i>wind-tunnel</i> setup. The top panel shows the formation of the shock and the evolution of the flow past the compact object embedded in the envelope of its companion massive star. Plotted is the density in units of &rho;<sub>&infin;</sub> in the orbital (x-y) plane of the binary with the white dot at the coordinate origin representing the embedded object. The lines with arrowheads in white represent velocity streamlines of the flow. The bottom panel tracks the evolution of the coefficients of accretion <i>C</i><sub>a</sub> and drag <i>C</i><sub>d</sub> as a function of time. The time quoted in the movie is in code units <i>R</i><sub>a</sub>/<i>v</i><sub>&infin;</sub>, where <i>R</i><sub>a</sub> is the accretion radius and <i>v</i><sub>&infin;</sub> is the relative velocity of the flow past the embedded object.</p>

<h1>
Fits
</h1>
</center>
<p>Presented below are three-dimensional interactive plots showing fitting relations between the coefficient of accretion <i>C</i><sub>a</sub> and coefficient of drag <i>C</i><sub>d</sub> in terms of the mass ratio <i>q</i> and upstream Mach number <i>M</i><sub>&infin;</sub> obtained on mapping the input parameters to the results from the hydrodynamical simulations.</p>

</h2>
</center>
